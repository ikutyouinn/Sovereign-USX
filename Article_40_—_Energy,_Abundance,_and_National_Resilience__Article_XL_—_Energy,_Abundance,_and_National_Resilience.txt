Section 1. Principle of Energy Abundance
 üîí
The Republic recognizes access to reliable, affordable, and sufficient energy as a foundational condition of economic vitality, public welfare, and national security.

Public policy shall favor energy abundance over enforced scarcity, except where temporary restraint is demonstrably necessary to prevent material harm, catastrophic failure, or system collapse.

Energy scarcity imposed by design, ideology, administrative convenience, or regulatory obstruction is forbidden.

Section 2. Primacy of Private Initiative
The production, innovation, ownership, and distribution of energy shall, as a default rule, be open to private individuals, cooperatives, and enterprises operating lawfully within the Republic.

Private energy generation‚Äîincluding decentralized, off-grid, experimental, and self-provisioned systems‚Äîis presumptively lawful.

The State shall not monopolize energy production, control pricing or allocation, suppress private innovation, or restrict lawful energy development absent clear and convincing evidence of material harm or systemic risk.

Section 3. Technology Neutrality and Deregulatory Mandate
 üîí
Energy policy shall be:

a. technology-neutral;
b. grounded in empirical evidence, reproducible analysis, and transparent risk assessment;
c. open to continuous innovation, improvement, and replacement.

No energy source‚Äîsolar, nuclear, fossil, geothermal, synthetic, or novel‚Äîshall be prohibited, penalized, subsidized, or favored by ideology, asserted consensus, or political narrative.

Regulatory barriers that materially obstruct private energy development, deployment, or modernization shall be removed unless the State demonstrates, by clear and convincing evidence, that such barriers are necessary to prevent concrete harm.

Section 4. Limited Public Works Authority (Exceptional and Temporary)

Where private provision is demonstrably incapable of meeting essential national needs due to scale, security, or catastrophic vulnerability‚Äîand where deregulation alone is insufficient‚Äîthe Republic may act directly as a measure of last resort.

Any public construction or operation of energy infrastructure shall require:

a. express statutory authorization;
b. written findings of demonstrated necessity;
c. full transparency and audit;
d. automatic sunset not exceeding five (5) years, absent supermajority renewal.

Public energy works shall not crowd out private enterprise, entrench obsolete systems, or persist once private provision becomes feasible.

Section 5. Corps of Engineers and Critical Infrastructure

Congress may authorize the Corps of Engineers or successor public works bodies to design or construct energy infrastructure only where:

a. national defense, disaster resilience, or grid survival so require;
b. failure to act would impose unacceptable risk to life or sovereignty;
c. private actors cannot reasonably perform the work in time or at scale.

Such authority shall be strictly limited, temporary, and shall not include pricing control, permanent operation, or market exclusion.

Section 6. Environmental and Human Balance

Energy development shall be governed consistently with:

a. Article IX (Land, Homesteading, and Agricultural Duty);
b. Article XXXVII (Environmental and Ecological Stewardship);
c. Article VIII (Economic Structure and Stewardship).

Environmental protection shall be evidence-based and proportional, and shall not serve as a pretext for energy deprivation, permanent austerity, or indirect control of the population.

Section 7. Austerity and Emergency Limitations
 üîí

Energy rationing, compulsory reduction, or enforced austerity may occur only where:

a. material scarcity or imminent system collapse is demonstrated by clear and convincing evidence;
b. measures are temporary, narrowly tailored, and judicially reviewable;
c. no less restrictive alternative can reasonably achieve the same protection.

Permanent emergency framing of energy governance is prohibited.

Section 8. Breakthrough Energy: Incentives, Disclosure, and Sunset

Where a method of energy generation is demonstrated to be civilization-scale in impact‚Äîcapable of materially eliminating energy scarcity through abundance‚Äîthe Republic shall encourage its disclosure and deployment through voluntary incentives rather than compulsion.

Congress may provide for statutory frameworks that:

a. offer substantial prizes, bounties, or guaranteed public procurement contracts for disclosure and deployment;
b. encourage voluntary licensing on fair and reasonable terms;
c. protect inventors against suppression, sabotage, or foreign capture.

Any such framework shall be evidence-based, non-punitive, and non-coercive, and may include:

d. optional shortened periods of exclusive rights;
e. optional post-recoupment licensing incentives, triggered only after demonstrable recovery of development costs and reasonable profit;
f. eventual public-domain release after defined temporal or economic thresholds, where continued exclusivity no longer serves innovation or public benefit.

No individual or entity shall be compelled to deploy, license, or surrender energy technology absent voluntary agreement or judicial finding of fraud, suppression, or unlawful restraint of trade.

Section 9. Anti-Subsidy and Anti-Cronyism Rule
 üîí

No subsidies, tax preferences, loan guarantees, or preferential financing shall be granted to any energy source or producer.

Public funds may be used only for neutral prizes, open procurement, or temporary public works authorized under Sections 4 or 5.

Cronyism, capture, and artificial market distortion are forbidden.

Section 10. Construction

This Article shall be interpreted to:

a. favor abundance, resilience, and innovation over scarcity and stagnation;
b. preserve maximum private liberty consistent with national survival;
c. reject ideological or technocratic capture of energy policy;
d. bind the State to the same standards of evidence, proportionality, and restraint imposed upon the People;
e. permit any person or enterprise with standing to challenge regulatory obstruction, unreasonable delay, or denial of lawful energy development as a form of artificial scarcity, subject to evidentiary standards and judicial review consistent with this Constitution.

Pursuant to Article XV, the following provisions of Article XL ‚Äî Energy, Abundance, and National Resilience are hereby designated Lock-Protected Provisions. These provisions may not be amended, repealed, suspended, or reinterpreted except by the supermajority and procedural safeguards specified in Article XV.
