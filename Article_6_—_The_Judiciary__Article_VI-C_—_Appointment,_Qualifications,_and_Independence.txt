Article VI-C — Appointment, Qualifications, and Independence

Section 1. Judicial Offices Covered

This Subarticle governs the appointment, qualifications, tenure, and independence of judges of:
	1.	the Supreme Court of the Republic;
	2.	the Constitutional Review Court; and
	3.	the Circuit and District Courts of the Republic,

except where this Constitution expressly provides otherwise.

⸻

Section 2. Judicial Confirmation Council

A Judicial Confirmation Council (JCC) is hereby established.

The Judicial Confirmation Council shall consist of seven (7) members, selected as follows:
	1.	two members appointed by the President;
	2.	two members appointed by the People’s House;
	3.	two members appointed by the Senate; and
	4.	one member selected by majority vote of the sitting Supreme Court.

Members of the Judicial Confirmation Council shall serve staggered terms as provided by law and shall not hold judicial office while serving.

The Council exists to evaluate professional competence, ethical fitness, and constitutional understanding, not political ideology.

No function assigned to the CJC may be exercised by the CRC, and no function assigned to the CRC may be exercised by the CJC, except where this Constitution expressly provides joint certification.
⸻

Section 3. Nomination and Appointment Process

Judges of the Supreme Court, Constitutional Review Court, and Circuit Courts shall be appointed through the following process:
	1.	nomination by the President;
	2.	vetting and public evaluation by the Judicial Confirmation Council; and
	3.	confirmation by a majority vote of the Senate.

District Court judges shall be appointed as provided by law, subject to constitutional qualification standards and Senate confirmation unless otherwise authorized by this Constitution.

No judicial appointment shall take effect without Senate confirmation.

⸻

Section 4. Qualifications

All judges shall:
	1.	be Civic Citizens of the Republic;
	2.	possess substantial legal training and experience as defined by law;
	3.	demonstrate competence in constitutional interpretation;
	4.	possess a record of ethical conduct and professional integrity.

Judges shall not be selected or excluded on the basis of race, sex, religion, ideology, political affiliation, or factional loyalty.

⸻

Section 5. Disqualifications

No person shall be appointed or serve as a judge if that person:
	1.	holds office in the Legislative or Executive Branch;
	2.	has served as a registered lobbyist, senior political operative, or campaign manager within the preceding five (5) years;
	3.	holds substantial financial interests presenting unresolved conflicts of interest;
	4.	is bound by oath, pledge, or allegiance to a foreign power or transnational authority;
	5.	has engaged in conduct demonstrating contempt for constitutional limits.

⸻

Section 6. Term of Office, Mobility, and Service Limits

Judges of the Supreme Court, the Constitutional Review Court, the Circuit Courts, and the District Courts shall be subject to a cumulative lifetime limit of twenty-one (21) years of judicial service across all judicial benches of the Republic.

Service on any judicial bench shall count toward this cumulative limit, regardless of rank or court.

Upon completion of service on a particular court, a judge may be appointed to a different judicial court, provided that:
	1.	the judge does not exceed the cumulative lifetime service limit;
	2.	the judge does not return to the same court or substantially identical role;
	3.	the appointment does not constitute promotion to a court of equal or greater constitutional authority after completion of service on a higher court.

No judge may serve more than once on the Supreme Court or the Constitutional Review Court.

No judicial appointment may be structured to evade or extend the lifetime service limit imposed by this Section.

⸻

Section 7. Compensation and Non-Diminution

Judicial compensation shall be fixed by law and shall not be diminished during a judge’s term of office.

Judges shall not receive compensation, gifts, or benefits from any source other than lawful public compensation.

⸻

Section 8. Independence and Immunity

Judges shall be independent in the exercise of their judicial duties.

No judge shall be subject to discipline, removal, or retaliation for lawful judicial decisions or good-faith constitutional interpretation.

Judicial immunity shall not extend to corruption, criminal conduct, or willful violation of this Constitution.

⸻

Section 9. Prohibitions on Influence

No person or entity shall:
	1.	attempt to coerce or improperly influence a judge;
	2.	condition confirmation on ideological pledges or commitments;
	3.	require party affiliation, loyalty oaths, or doctrinal conformity.

Judges shall not engage in political fundraising, partisan advocacy, or public endorsement of candidates while in office.

⸻

Section 10. Cooling-Off and Post-Service Restrictions

Upon leaving judicial office, former judges shall be subject to cooling-off periods as provided by law.

No former judge may appear before the court on which they served for a period not less than five (5) years following separation.

⸻

Section 11. Interpretation

This Subarticle shall be interpreted to secure judicial independence, prevent ideological capture, preserve public confidence in the courts, and ensure that judges serve the Constitution rather than faction or power.

⸻
