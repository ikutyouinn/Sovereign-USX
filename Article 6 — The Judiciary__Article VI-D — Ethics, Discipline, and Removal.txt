Article VI-D — Ethics, Discipline, and Removal

(Authoritative, Integrated Version)

Section 1. Judicial Duty and Ethical Obligation

Judicial office is a public trust.

Judges shall uphold the Constitution, apply the law faithfully, and conduct themselves with integrity, impartiality, and restraint.

⸻

Section 2. Code of Judicial Ethics

A uniform Code of Judicial Ethics shall govern conflicts of interest, disclosure, recusal, conduct, and post-service restrictions.

No ethical rule shall punish lawful judicial reasoning.

⸻

Section 3. Judicial Review Board

A Judicial Review Board (JRB) is hereby established as an independent body.

Members shall be appointed by the President, the Legislature, and the Judiciary in balanced proportions and shall not hold judicial office concurrently.

⸻

Section 4. Jurisdiction and Authority

The JRB may investigate credible allegations of misconduct, conduct hearings with due process, issue findings of fact, and recommend discipline or removal.

The JRB shall not discipline judges for the substance of lawful judicial decisions.

The Constitutional Judicial Commission shall have concurrent authority to refer evidence of corruption, capture, retaliation, or systemic misconduct to the Judicial Review Board.

The JRB shall not decline or dismiss a CJC referral without written findings subject to public disclosure.

⸻

Section 5. Grounds for Discipline

Judicial discipline may be imposed only for:
	1.	corruption or bribery;
	2.	willful violation of this Constitution;
	3.	criminal conduct;
	4.	persistent failure to perform judicial duties;
	5.	abuse of office;
	6.	knowing and material ethical violations;
	7.	willful or repeated bench legislation, as defined in Article VI-A.

Disagreement with legal reasoning alone shall not constitute grounds for discipline.

⸻

Section 6. Sanctions

Sanctions may include reprimand, suspension, reassignment, or removal.

Judicial officers who engage in willful or repeated bench legislation shall be presumed unfit for continued judicial service, absent clear and convincing evidence of isolated error promptly corrected upon review.

⸻

Section 7. Removal from Office

Removal requires a finding of cause by the Judicial Review Board and confirmation by a supermajority of the Senate, or such other process as this Constitution expressly authorizes.

⸻

Section 8. Interim Measures

Temporary suspension may occur where necessary to protect judicial integrity and shall not be punitive.

⸻

Section 9. Protection Against Retaliation

No judge shall be disciplined for lawful decisions, good-faith constitutional interpretation, or dissenting opinions.

Retaliatory discipline is void.

⸻

Section 10. Transparency and Records

Disciplinary findings and sanctions shall be public and preserved pursuant to Article VI-E and Article XXXIX.

⸻

Section 11. Emergency Constraint

No emergency or continuity doctrine shall suspend ethical accountability.

⸻

Section 12. Interpretation

This Subarticle shall be interpreted to ensure accountability without intimidation and independence without impunity.

⸻
