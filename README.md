## Why this exists

This project began as an attempt to explain my political views to a sister who believed I was a partisan loyalist. Well, not exactly...  

Anyway, frustrated by having my positions collapsed into bumper-sticker labels and left versus right labels, by many of the people with whom I would share opinions and critiques of the government and society, I decided instead to describe what a government would actually look like if I were responsible for designing it.

What started as a few paragraphs to explain my positions began to grow into what looked like a rant. I thought rants were useless; so, I moved on to drafting what were initially proposed amendments to the United States Constitution. Eventually that approach also fell short, and I began to design a complete and internally coherent constitutional system.

This framework borrows selectively from classical liberalism (Enlightenment, Founding Fathers), libertarian political theory, contemporary public discourse (including suggestions from users on X), and even fictional sources; though I swear I have never read Starship Troopers. Perhaps I should. Anyhow, I came up with many ideas on my own, based on observed problems and thoughts about how I would solve them. The underlying theory is that politics and government are better solved as an engineering problem.

Much of the initial text was generated with the assistance of AI. Producing this volume of material manually would have required months of work and the coordinated effort of multiple contributors. Though LLMs were great at generating small structures and pointing out incoherence, much text would also be eaten by the collapse of the context windows.

Frustrated, again, I decided that I was going to share the ideas, anywayâ€”so why not share authorship and, I hope, get some bright minds to help me flesh it out and filter out the parts that are poorly conceived, overly onerous, or just silly? The goal was never purity, but conceptual exploration: testing structures, stress-testing assumptions, and identifying failure modes in modern governance, then trying to solve them like an engineer.

## Current state

The project remains unfinished and imperfect. Certain componentsâ€”particularly those related to Civic Citizenshipâ€”are likely to be reworked into a point-based or weighted voting system rather than a strict binary earned status. Nevertheless, I am publishing the work in its current form, errors and meme fodder included.

An endless pursuit of perfection has already consumed hundreds of hours, and further isolation risks never completing the project. By releasing these ideas publicly, I hope to invite critique, refinement, and parallel experimentation, allowing stronger ideas to surface, spread, and improve, alongside incorporating new ideas.
